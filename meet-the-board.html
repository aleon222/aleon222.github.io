Cerissa A. Brown

Board Chair

Cerissa A. Brown, Chair of the Board of Directors at Reecee's Reach, is a passionate advocate and catalyst for change in Black women's mental health. With over 15 years of experience in the field, she deeply understands the unique challenges Black women face and is committed to providing them with the resources and support they need to thrive.

Cerissa's commitment to mental health is evident in her work. In her 2021 Mayoral campaign in Birmingham, Alabama, she made mental health a cornerstone issue. As Chair of the Board at Reecee's Reach, she envisions the organization as a sanctuary for Black women, providing equitable and effective mental health resources and support. She is confident that with the concerted efforts of the Board and the community, Reecee's Reach can make a significant difference in the lives of Black women.

Cerissa is also a sought-after speaker, enlightening various events, seminars, and discussions with her insights. Academically, she also excels, pursuing a Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) at Walden University with a perfect 4.0 GPA. She is a proud member of three honor societies and a student member of the National Association of Social Workers (NASW).

Darcel Burton

Board Co-Chair

At just 23, Darcel Burton has already significantly impacted her community as Co-Chair of the Board of Directors at Reecee's Reach. She was born into a family with a long history of community service, and her commitment to social justice is evident in everything she does.

In addition to her work at Reecee's Reach, Darcel is also a passionate advocate for cultural diversity and understanding. She has tirelessly researched and confronted social challenges and deeply respects Black women's strength and resilience.

As Co-Chair of Reecee's Reach, Darcel celebrates stories from all Black women with mental health challenges. She desires to educate, inspire, and deepen the community's understanding of the diverse perspectives that shape our world.

Glady Brown

Board Member

Loretta Gladys Brown is a compelling voice of experience and advocacy on the Reecee's Reach Board of Directors. Guided by Romans 5:8, 'But God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us,' she brings a unique perspective to the organization, informed by her experience with mental health.

Loretta's personal history of overcoming resilience and faith inspires her advocacy. As a struggling single mom and survivor of domestic abuse, poverty, and homelessness, her life is a testament to hope. "I'm thankful to Jesus for allowing me to use my past experiences to point others to Him who can do exceedingly abundantly above all that we ask or think," she says about her vision for her role on the board.

Loretta's commitment to community advocacy is evident in initiatives like the MLK Day of Service and Feminine Hygiene Drive, reflecting her genuine understanding of marginalized communities' challenges. As a Community Member on the board, she voluntarily shares her faith-rooted insights and experiences to contribute to the organization's strategy and initiatives. Loretta takes her role seriously, providing valuable input on mental health initiatives, outreach, and awareness-raising activities and participating actively in decision-making.

Loretta sees her board position as not just a role but a calling. She is deeply committed to Reecee's Reach, envisioning the organization as a hand extended to Black women in need. "I see Reecee's Reach reaching women who need a helping hand," she reflects. "One day, they will be in a good place in life where they can always reach out and give a helping hand to someone else."
